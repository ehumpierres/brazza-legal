📱 What is Brazza?

Brazza is a free iPhone app that turns museum visits into scavenger hunts for families.

How it works: Parent downloads app → Kid picks a character guide → App gives clues to find real art → Family hunts together → They discover "hidden treasure" at the end!

Think of it as: Pokémon Go meets museum education. Kids lead, parents follow.

Key points: ✅ FREE · ✅ Works at The Met · ✅ 30-60 min · ✅ English & Spanish · ✅ iPhone only

💬 Opening Scripts

For Kids (get them excited!)

"Want to find a hidden treasure inside the museum?"
"Do you like scavenger hunts? There's one inside with real clues!"

For Parents

"Visiting with kids today? This keeps them actually engaged the whole time."
"It's like Pokémon Go but for art. And it's free."

Quick One-Liners (busy traffic)

"Free scavenger hunt for kids! Just scan the code."

✅ Best Practices

✅ DO

  • Lead with the kid — excite them first
  • Say "free" early — removes hesitation
  • Hand flyer to parent — they have the phone
  • Keep moving — walk alongside, don't block
  • Target people entering — not leaving
  • Smile! — you're giving them something fun

🚫 DON'T

  • Be pushy or aggressive
  • Block sidewalk or stairs
  • Argue if someone says no
  • Give flyers to people leaving
  • Stand still — stay active!
  • Waste flyers on adults without kids

❓ FAQs

"How does it work?"
Download the app, pick a guide, follow clues to find real art. Kids search, you hold the phone.
"Is it free?"
Yes, completely free. No hidden costs.
"Can I use it with both kids?"
Yes! Whole family plays together on one phone.
"Does my kid handle the phone?"
Nope — you hold it, they do the hunting.
"How long does it take?"
About 30-60 minutes depending on the hunt.
"Does it work on Android?"
Not yet — iPhone only for now.
"Is it in Spanish?"
Yes! English and Spanish available.
